For your 6-year-old son, we can design a learning plan that focuses on age-appropriate concepts and skills, building the foundation in key subjects like **Mathematics**, **English**, **Science**, **Social Studies**, **Art**, and **Life Skills**. Here's a breakdown:

---

### **Mathematics Bi-Weekly Plan for 6-Year-Old**

| **Topic**                        | **Skills to be Learned**                                                                  | **Resources for Teaching**                                                  | **Resources for Assessment**                                       | **Week 1-2**                                       | **Week 3-4**                                                 | **Week 5-6**                                                   |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Number Recognition**           | - Recognizing numbers and their order.                                                    | - [Khan Academy - Early Math](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/early-math)  | - [IXL - Kindergarten Math](https://www.ixl.com/math/kindergarten) | Practice identifying numbers 1-20.                 | Count numbers from 1 to 100.                                 | Introduce skip counting (by 2s, 5s, 10s).                      |
| **Basic Addition & Subtraction** | - Understanding addition and subtraction using visual aids (objects, fingers).            | - [ABCmouse - Math](https://www.abcmouse.com)                               | - [Prodigy Math Game](https://www.prodigygame.com)                 | Add and subtract numbers using visual aids.        | Practice addition and subtraction with simple equations.     | Use objects or drawings to represent addition and subtraction. |
| **Counting**                     | - Counting forwards and backwards, recognizing quantities.                                | - [Khan Academy - Counting](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/early-math)    | - [IXL - Counting](https://www.ixl.com/math/kindergarten)          | Count everyday objects (e.g., fruits, toys).       | Count in sets of 10s (10, 20, 30, etc.).                     | Practice counting backwards from 20 to 1.                      |
| **Shapes & Patterns**            | - Recognizing 2D shapes (circle, square, triangle, etc.) and simple patterns.             | - [PBS Kids - Shapes](https://pbskids.org)                                  | - [Quizlet - Shapes](https://quizlet.com)                          | Identify and name basic shapes.                    | Create patterns using shapes (e.g., circle, square, circle). | Recognize patterns in objects or drawings.                     |
| **Measurement (Simple)**         | - Understand basic measurements like length, weight, and volume (using hands-on objects). | - [Khan Academy - Measurement](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/early-math) | - [IXL - Measurement](https://www.ixl.com/math/kindergarten)       | Measure length using a ruler or tape.              | Compare weight using everyday objects.                       | Practice measuring volume using cups and spoons.               |
| **Time**                         | - Recognize the concept of time (morning, afternoon, evening) and the clock.              | - [Khan Academy - Time](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/early-math)        | - [IXL - Time](https://www.ixl.com/math/kindergarten)              | Learn to recognize times on the hour (e.g., 1:00). | Recognize times on half-past the hour.                       | Practice reading a simple analog clock.                        |

### **English Language & Literacy Bi-Weekly Plan for 6-Year-Old**

| **Topic**                   | **Skills to be Learned**                                             | **Resources for Teaching**                             | **Resources for Assessment**                                  | **Week 1-2**                                     | **Week 3-4**                                             | **Week 5-6**                                               |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Phonics & Letter Sounds** | - Learn letter sounds (A-Z) and recognize their use in simple words. | - [Starfall - Phonics](https://www.starfall.com)       | - [ABCmouse - Phonics](https://www.abcmouse.com)              | Learn sounds of letters A-Z.                     | Practice blending sounds to form words (cat, bat, etc.). | Start using simple CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) words.  |
| **Sight Words**             | - Recognize and read basic sight words.                              | - [SightWords.com](https://www.sightwords.com)         | - [Quizlet - Sight Words](https://quizlet.com)                | Learn common sight words (the, and, is, etc.).   | Use sight words in simple sentences.                     | Practice reading short sentences with sight words.         |
| **Reading Comprehension**   | - Understand the story or main idea of short texts.                  | - [Storynory](https://www.storynory.com/)              | - [Khan Academy - Reading](https://www.khanacademy.org/ela)   | Read short stories and ask questions about them. | Retell the story in their own words.                     | Focus on identifying the main character and setting.       |
| **Writing Skills**          | - Write letters, short words, and simple sentences.                  | - [Handwriting Without Tears](https://www.hwtears.com) | - [Writing Worksheets](https://www.education.com/worksheets/) | Write names, simple words.                       | Write simple sentences using sight words.                | Create simple stories or sentences using a picture prompt. |
| **Vocabulary Building**     | - Learn new words through context, stories, and games.               | - [PBS Kids - Vocabulary](https://pbskids.org)         | - [Quizlet - Vocabulary Flashcards](https://quizlet.com)      | Introduce 5 new words every week.                | Use new vocabulary in sentences.                         | Review vocabulary in stories and daily conversations.      |

### **Science Bi-Weekly Plan for 6-Year-Old**

| **Topic**            | **Skills to be Learned**                                               | **Resources for Teaching**                                           | **Resources for Assessment**                                | **Week 1-2**                                    | **Week 3-4**                                                | **Week 5-6**                                         |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Plants & Animals** | - Recognizing different types of plants and animals.                   | - [Khan Academy - Life Science](https://www.khanacademy.org/science) | - [Quizlet - Animals and Plants](https://quizlet.com)       | Study plants and flowers in the garden.         | Learn about farm animals and pets.                          | Explore animal habitats and where they live.         |
| **The Weather**      | - Understanding weather patterns (sunny, rainy, cloudy, windy).        | - [PBS Kids - Weather](https://pbskids.org)                          | - [Quizizz - Weather Quiz](https://quizizz.com)             | Learn to identify different weather conditions. | Discuss how weather changes in different seasons.           | Create a simple weather chart.                       |
| **The Five Senses**  | - Understanding the five senses (sight, hearing, taste, touch, smell). | - [Khan Academy - The Senses](https://www.khanacademy.org/science)   | - [IXL - Science](https://www.ixl.com/science/kindergarten) | Explore the senses using real-world examples.   | Test different objects using the senses (e.g., taste test). | Create a simple chart of favorite tastes and smells. |
| **Simple Machines**  | - Introduction to basic machines (lever, pulley, wheel).               | - [Science for Kids](https://www.scienceforkids.com)                 | - [Quizlet - Simple Machines](https://quizlet.com)          | Explore pulleys and levers with toys or tools.  | Build simple machines using household items.                | Discuss how simple machines help in everyday life.   |

### **Life Skills Bi-Weekly Plan for 6-Year-Old**

| **Topic**            | **Skills to be Learned**                                           | **Resources for Teaching**                                  | **Resources for Assessment**                           | **Week 1-2**                               | **Week 3-4**                                   | **Week 5-6**                                |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |

| **Safety Rules**     | - Understanding basic safety (stranger danger, crossing the road). | - [Safety Kids](https://www.safekids.org)                   | - [Quizlet - Safety Rules](https://quizlet.com)        | Discuss road safety and looking both ways. | Teach the importance of asking for permission. | Learn emergency numbers and when to call    |
